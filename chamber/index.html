Ozonolysis of Alkenes

⏻ Learning Objectives

At the end of this lab you will be

  • familiar with environmental chamber design and operation

  • explain the principle of operation of O3O_3O3​ and NOxNO_xNOx​ gas-phase monitors

  • able to carry out VOC oxidation experiments

  • able to estimate reaction rates from experimental data

  • able to explain the formation of secondary organic aerosol

  • able to contextualize measurements within the peer-reviewed literature

⌨ Prompt

The group will prepare a mix of O3O_3O3​, NOxNO_xNOx​, and reactive alkene inside an environmental chamber and monitor the formation and decay of O3O_3O3​ with time. You should first add NONONO, and turn on the light. Observe the formation of O3O_3O3​ and the photostationary state of the system. Then add the alkene and observe the change in O3O_3O3​ and NOxNO_xNOx​. Before the experiment, you will receive reagents from your TA, including a glass bulb with NONONO, and a vial with an alkene. This will allow you to use the decay of O3O_3O3​ to estimate the reaction kinetics. You should investigate the change of NONONO and NO2NO_2NO2​, but realize that the rate will be not only determined by SCI + NONONO and SCI + NO2NO_2NO2​, but also by additional radical reactions occurring in the chamber. For the report you should quantitatively evaluate the formation of O3O_3O3​ in the photostationary state, detail the origin of alkenes in the atmosphere, the alkene ozonolysis mechanism, estimate products, estimate the reaction rate of the alkene with ozone, and of the stable Crigee intermediate with NO and NO2NO_2NO2​. Contrast the rate with values found in the peer-reviewed literature. Explain the principle of operation of the gas-phase monitors. Discuss the role of wall loss and other potential experimental artifacts that may bias your results. Also discuss the implication for secondary organic aerosol formation, including estimates of particulate matter mass that may have formed in the you reaction.

This lab uses an environmental chamber, a photometric UV absorption O3O_3O3​ analyzer and a chemiluminescence NOxNO_xNOx​ analyzer. To measure O3O_3O3​, a 254 nm UV light signal is passed through the sample cell where it is absorbed in proportion to the amount of ozone present. Periodically, a switching valve alternates measurement between the sample stream and a sample that has been scrubbed of ozone. The NOxNO_xNOx​ instrument determines the concentration of nitric oxide (NONONO), total nitrogen oxides (NOxNO_xNOx​) , the sum of NONONO and NO2NO_2NO2​) and nitrogen dioxide (NO2NO_2NO2​) in a sample stream. The principle of operation is chemiluminescence. Chemiluminescence is the emission of light from a chemical reaction and is triggered by the reaction of NONONO with ozone O3O_3O3​. The amount of light produced is linear with NONONO concentration. NO2NO_2NO2​ is measured by converting NO2NO_2NO2​ with to NONONO using heated molybdenum converter chip.

Alkene Oxidation

Alkenes are ubiquitous atmospheric VOCs that originate from both biogenic and anthropogenic sources. The double bond reacts quickly with O3O_3O3​ and is one of the major degradation pathways of alkenes. The ozone alkene reaction starts with the O3O_3O3​ addition to the double bond resulting in a primary ozonide. The primary ozonide decomposes into an aldehyde and a stabilized Crigee intermediate (SCI) biradical. The SCI then reacts with either (a) an adehyde, (b) an alcohol, (d) carbon monoxide, (e) sulfur dioxide, (f) water vapor, (g) NONONO or NO2NO_2NO2​, or (h) with itself. Some of these compounds will then go on and contribute to PM2.5. Furthermore hydroxyl radicals are formed from the reaction at high yield. The alkene + O3O_3O3​ reaction is therefore important for understanding the degradation and fate of alkenes in the atmosphere, for understanding the OH budget in the atmosphere, and for understanding PM2.5 formation from VOCs in the atmosphere.

Source. Suda et al. (2012, doi:10.1029/2011JD016823).
