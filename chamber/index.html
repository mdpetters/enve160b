Ozonolysis of Alkenes

⏻ Learning Objectives

At the end of this lab you will be

  • familiar with environmental chamber design and operation

  • explain the principle of operation of O3O_3O3​ and NOxNO_xNOx​ gas-phase monitors

  • able to carry out VOC oxidation experiments

  • able to estimate reaction rates from experimental data

  • able to explain the formation of secondary organic aerosol

  • able to contextualize measurements within the peer-reviewed literature

⌨ Prompt
The goal is to measure the reactions of alkenes with ozone inside an environmental chamber. The group will prepare a mix of O3O_3O3​, NOxNO_xNOx​, and reactive alkene inside an environmental chamber and monitor the decay of O3O_3O3​ with time. You should first add O3O_3O3​, NOxNO_xNOx​, and turn on the light. Then add 100 ppb of alkene. For the report you should detail the origin of alkenes in the atmosphere, the alkene ozonolysis mechanism, estimate products, estimate the reaction rate of the alkene with ozone, and of the stable Crigee intermediate with NO and NO2NO_2NO2​. Contrast the rate with values found in the peer-reviewed literature. Explain the principle of operation of the gas-phase monitors. Discuss the role of wall loss and other potential experimental artifacts that may bias your results. Also discuss the implication for secondary organic aerosol formation, including estimates of particulate matter mass that may have formed in the you reaction.

This lab uses an environmental chamber, an ozone sensor and a chemiluminescence analyzer. The instrument determines the concentration of nitric oxide (NONONO), total nitrogen oxides (NOxNO_xNOx​) , the sum of NONONO and NO2NO_2NO2​) and nitrogen dioxide (NO2NO_2NO2​) in a sample stream. The principle of operation is chemiluminescence. Chemiluminescence is the emission of light from a chemical reaction and is triggered by the reaction of NONONO with ozone O3O_3O3​. The amount of light produced is linear with NONONO concentration. NO2NO_2NO2​ is measured by converting NO2NO_2NO2​ with to NONONO using heated molybdenum converter chip.

Background/Motivation

Alkenes are ubiquitous atmospheric VOCs that originate from both biogenic and anthropogenic sources. The double bond reacts quickly with O3O_3O3​ and is one of the major degradation pathways of alkenes. The ozone alkene reaction starts with the O3O_3O3​ addition to the double bond resulting in a primary ozonide. The primary ozonide decomposes into an aldehyde and a stabilized Crigee intermediate (SCI) biradical. The SCI then reacts with either (a) an adehyde, (b) an alcohol, (d) carbon monoxide, (e) sulfur dioxide, (f) water vapor, (g) NONONO or NO2NO_2NO2​, or (h) with itself. Some of these compounds are will then go on and contribute to PM2.5. Furthermore hydroxyl radicals are formed from the reaction at high yield. The alkene + O3O_3O3​ reaction is therefore important for understanding the degradation and fate of alkenes in the atmosphere, for understanding the OH budget in the atmosphere, and for understanding PM2.5 formation from VOCs in the atmosphere.

Source. Suda et al. (2012, doi:10.1029/2011JD016823).

Before the experiment, you will receive reagents from your TA, including a glass bulb with O3O_3O3​, a glass bulb with NONONO, an empty glass bulb, and a vial with an alkene, and a vial with butanol. The O3O_3O3​ and NONONO bulbs will be pre-measured for you to give a concentration of ~500 ppb of O3O_3O3​ and ~200 ppb of NONONO. You need to calculate the liquid volume of alkene and butanol to add to the bulb and then chamber. The target amount is ~100 ppb of alkene and ~200 ppb of butanol. Butanol will not react with O3O_3O3​, but will react with OHOHOH radicals formed during ozonolysis. This will allow you to use the decay of O3O_3O3​ to estimate the reaction kinetics. You should investigate the decay of NONONO and NO2NO_2NO2​, but realize that the rate will be not only determined by SCI + NONONO and SCI + NO2NO_2NO2​, but also by additional radical reactions occurring in the chamber.
